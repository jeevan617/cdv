‚ö†Ô∏è Moderate Diabetic Retinopathy

Moderate changes detected. Consultation with your ophthalmologist is important to develop a comprehensive treatment plan.

Medical Consultation Required

  • Ophthalmologist Consultation

    Schedule an appointment with your ophthalmologist for comprehensive evaluation and treatment planning.

  • Frequent Monitoring

    More frequent eye exams (every 3-6 months) may be recommended to monitor progression.

  • Optimize Diabetes Control

    Work closely with your healthcare team to achieve optimal blood sugar, blood pressure, and cholesterol levels.

  • Consider Treatment Options

    Discuss potential treatments with your doctor, which may include laser therapy or injections if progression occurs.

  • Protect Your Vision

    Avoid activities that may increase eye pressure. Report any vision changes immediately.

{% elif level == 3 %}

üö® Severe Diabetic Retinopathy

Severe retinopathy detected. Immediate consultation with a retinal specialist is strongly recommended to prevent vision loss.

Urgent Recommendations

  • See a Retinal Specialist

    Schedule an urgent appointment with a retinal specialist for comprehensive evaluation and treatment.

  • Treatment Planning

    Discuss treatment options including laser photocoagulation, anti-VEGF injections, or other interventions.

  • Intensive Diabetes Management

    Work with your endocrinologist to achieve the best possible glycemic control immediately.

  • Frequent Monitoring

    Expect frequent follow-up appointments to monitor disease progression and treatment response.

  • Report Vision Changes

    Immediately report any sudden vision changes, floaters, or flashes of light to your eye doctor.

{% elif level == 4 %}

üö® Proliferative Diabetic Retinopathy - Urgent

Advanced diabetic retinopathy detected. This is a serious condition requiring immediate medical attention to prevent severe vision loss.

Immediate Action Required

  • Emergency Consultation

    Contact a retinal specialist immediately for urgent evaluation and treatment. Do not delay.

  • Treatment Intervention

    You will likely need prompt treatment such as laser surgery (panretinal photocoagulation) or anti-VEGF injections.

  • Vitrectomy Consideration

    In some cases, vitrectomy surgery may be necessary. Discuss all options with your retinal specialist.

  • Aggressive Diabetes Control

    Work intensively with your diabetes care team to optimize blood sugar, blood pressure, and overall health.

  • Vision Protection

    Avoid heavy lifting, straining, or activities that increase intraocular pressure. Seek immediate care for any vision changes.

  • Close Follow-up

    Expect very frequent monitoring and follow-up appointments to track treatment progress.
